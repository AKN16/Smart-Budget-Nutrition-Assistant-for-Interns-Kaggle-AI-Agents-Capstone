> This meal plan prioritizes a balanced intake of macronutrients by featuring rice as a consistent complex carbohydrate base. Protein sources are varied, including eggs, chicken, pork, tofu, and fish, ensuring a good amino acid profile. A wide array of local vegetables like morning glory, winter melon, cabbage, and tomatoes are incorporated daily, providing essential vitamins, minerals, and fiber. While the budget is tight, the plan aims for variety in preparation methods and ingredients to prevent monotony and support overall nutritional well-being.

## Money-Saving Tips
- Shop at Local Wet Markets (Chợ): Markets like Chợ Bà Chiểu or smaller neighborhood markets offer significantly cheaper fresh produce, meat, and fish than supermarkets. Go in the late afternoon for discounts, but earlier for best selection.
- Embrace Seasonal and Local Produce: Stick to vegetables and fruits that are in season and locally abundant, as they will be the freshest and cheapest. Morning glory, water spinach, cabbage, and various gourds are often very affordable.
- Cook in Batches and Utilize Leftovers: Prepare larger portions of dishes like braised pork or stir-fries that can be reheated for lunch the next day, saving time and reducing food waste.
- Limit Processed Foods and Sugary Drinks: These items are often expensive relative to their nutritional value and can quickly deplete a budget. Stick to water, and make your own simple desserts with fruit.
- Bargain (Politely) at Markets: While not always possible in supermarkets, polite bargaining at local wet markets can sometimes save you a few thousand VND, especially when buying multiple items from the same vendor.
